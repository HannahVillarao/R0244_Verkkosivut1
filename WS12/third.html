The Solar System

The gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it.

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System. It provides the energy that supports life on Earth and controls the orbits of the planets.

Mercury: The Closest Planet

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. It has extreme temperature variations because it has no atmosphere to retain heat.

Venus: The Hot Planet

Venus is similar in size to Earth, but its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide makes it the hottest planet in the Solar System.

Earth: Our Home

Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has a rich atmosphere, liquid water, and moderate temperatures that sustain life.

Mars: The Red Planet

Mars, the Red Planet, has a dusty, rocky surface. It is the most Earth-like planet and has been the focus of many space exploration missions.

Jupiter: The Giant Planet

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. Its Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has lasted for centuries.

Saturn: The Ringed Planet

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system made of ice, rock, and dust. It is a gas giant with a series of moons and rings.

Uranus: The Tilted Planets

Uranus rotates on its side, making its seasons extreme. It is a gas giant with a faint ring system and a blue-green color.

Neptune The Cold Blue PLanet

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It has intense winds, the fastest in the Solar System, and a mysterious dark spot.
